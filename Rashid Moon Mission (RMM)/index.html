The Rashid Rover

Built in the UAE

Rashid Rover was developed by the Mohammed Bin Rashid Space Centre (MBRSC). Weighing just 10 kg, it was equipped with four cameras, a microscopic imager, a Langmuir probe, and a thermal imaging camera to study the lunar surface and plasma environment.

Mission Log

Mission Timeline

September 2017
Mission Announced
UAE announces its first lunar mission at the International Astronautical Congress, aiming to land a rover on the Moon.
2019–2021
Rover Development
MBRSC engineers design, build, and test the Rashid Rover entirely in the UAE, with international science partnerships.
December 11, 2022
Launch
Rashid Rover launches aboard the ispace Hakuto-R Mission 1 lander on a SpaceX Falcon 9 from Cape Canaveral.
April 25, 2023
Landing Attempt
The Hakuto-R lander attempts touchdown at Atlas Crater, Mare Frigoris. Contact is lost during final descent — a hard landing occurs.
Post-Mission
Legacy & Future
Despite the outcome, UAE space officials declare the mission a success for learning. Mission 2 is planned with upgraded systems and greater autonomy.
